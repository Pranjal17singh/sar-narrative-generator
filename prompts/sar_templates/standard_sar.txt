SAR NARRATIVE TEMPLATE

A Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) narrative should be clear, concise, and provide sufficient detail for law enforcement to understand the suspicious activity without needing additional documentation.

STRUCTURE:

1. INTRODUCTION
Begin with identifying information about the subject and the reason for filing. State the type of suspicious activity and the general time period.

Example opening:
"This Suspicious Activity Report is being filed concerning [SUBJECT NAME], account holder of [ACCOUNT TYPE] account number [ACCOUNT NUMBER], regarding suspicious transactions totaling approximately $[AMOUNT] between [START DATE] and [END DATE]."

2. SUBJECT BACKGROUND
Provide relevant background about the subject, including:
- Account opening date and stated purpose
- Expected account activity
- Previous suspicious activity (if any)
- Any relevant KYC information that contrasts with observed activity

3. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY DESCRIPTION
Describe the suspicious activity in chronological order:
- Specific dates, amounts, and transaction types
- Counterparties involved (if known)
- Geographic locations relevant to the activity
- Any patterns observed (structuring, rapid movement, etc.)

Use objective language. State what was observed, not conclusions about intent.

4. RED FLAGS AND INDICATORS
List the specific red flags that triggered the SAR:
- Unusual transaction patterns
- Inconsistencies with stated business purpose
- Known money laundering typologies
- Geographic risk factors

5. SUPPORTING INFORMATION
Include any additional relevant information:
- Customer explanations (if any)
- Results of enhanced due diligence
- Related accounts or parties
- Previous SARs filed (reference numbers if available)

6. CONCLUSION
Summarize the suspicious activity and note any actions taken by the institution, such as:
- Account restrictions
- Relationship termination
- Ongoing monitoring

WRITING GUIDELINES:

- Use clear, professional language
- Be specific with amounts, dates, and parties
- Avoid legal conclusions (e.g., "money laundering")
- State facts and observations
- Include all relevant details in the narrative itself
- Reference supporting documentation where appropriate
