COMMON MONEY LAUNDERING TYPOLOGIES AND RED FLAGS

1. STRUCTURING (SMURFING)
Definition: Breaking up transactions to avoid Currency Transaction Report (CTR) requirements.
Red Flags:
- Multiple cash deposits just below $10,000
- Deposits at multiple branches on same day
- Multiple individuals making deposits to same account
- Transactions just below reporting thresholds
Narrative Language: "Multiple deposits structured below the $10,000 CTR threshold..."

2. FUNNEL ACCOUNTS
Definition: Accounts receiving deposits from multiple sources, then transferring to single destination.
Red Flags:
- Deposits from numerous unrelated parties
- Quick transfers out after deposits
- Little or no account balance maintained
- Wire transfers to high-risk jurisdictions
Narrative Language: "The account received funds from multiple apparently unrelated parties, which were then consolidated and transferred..."

3. RAPID MOVEMENT OF FUNDS
Definition: Quick movement of funds through accounts with minimal retention.
Red Flags:
- Large deposits immediately followed by withdrawals
- Pass-through activity
- No apparent business purpose
- Minimal time between deposit and withdrawal
Narrative Language: "Funds were moved through the account rapidly, typically within 24-48 hours of receipt..."

4. ROUND DOLLAR TRANSACTIONS
Definition: Unusual frequency of transactions in round amounts.
Red Flags:
- Multiple transactions in exact round amounts ($5,000, $10,000, etc.)
- Pattern inconsistent with normal business activity
- Combined with other suspicious indicators
Narrative Language: "A pattern of round-dollar transactions was observed, including..."

5. SHELL COMPANY ACTIVITY
Definition: Use of shell companies to obscure ownership and source of funds.
Red Flags:
- Transactions with companies in secrecy jurisdictions
- No apparent legitimate business purpose
- Minimal online presence or business footprint
- Complex ownership structures
Narrative Language: "Transactions were conducted with entities that appear to lack legitimate business purpose..."

6. TRADE-BASED MONEY LAUNDERING
Definition: Manipulation of trade transactions to transfer value.
Red Flags:
- Over/under invoicing
- Multiple invoicing for same goods
- Phantom shipments
- Misrepresentation of goods/services
Narrative Language: "Transaction patterns suggest possible trade-based value transfer..."

7. LAYERING THROUGH MULTIPLE ACCOUNTS
Definition: Moving funds through multiple accounts to obscure the trail.
Red Flags:
- Transfers between multiple accounts
- Use of intermediary accounts
- Cross-border wire activity
- Complex transaction chains
Narrative Language: "Funds were transferred through multiple accounts in apparent layering activity..."

8. HIGH-RISK JURISDICTION ACTIVITY
Definition: Transactions with countries known for weak AML controls.
Red Flags:
- Wire transfers to/from high-risk countries
- Inconsistent with customer's stated business
- No apparent economic purpose
- Offshore financial centers
Narrative Language: "Significant transaction activity was observed with high-risk jurisdictions including..."

9. CASH-INTENSIVE BUSINESS ANOMALIES
Definition: Cash patterns inconsistent with stated business type.
Red Flags:
- Cash deposits inconsistent with business type
- Increasing cash activity without business growth
- Geographic inconsistencies
Narrative Language: "Cash transaction patterns were inconsistent with the customer's stated business..."

10. VELOCITY CHANGES
Definition: Sudden changes in transaction patterns or volumes.
Red Flags:
- Dramatic increase in transaction volume
- Change in transaction types
- New counterparties
- Activity inconsistent with history
Narrative Language: "A significant change in account activity was observed, with transaction volume increasing..."
